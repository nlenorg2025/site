Creating a community that works.

Since 1999, NLEN has provided a holistic approach to workforce development. We connect residents of North Lawndale and beyond to employment, training, and financial tools that lead to economic mobility, and brighter futures.

MISSION + IMPACT

Building careers. Restoring dignity. Strengthening community.

What began as a community-led initiative guided by the Steans Family Foundation has grown into a trusted leader in workforce development, reentry services, and social enterprises. Our holistic approach includes job readiness training, transitional employment, industry-specific skills programs, and wraparound financial services.

In 2004, NLEN launched Sweet Beginnings: our award-winning social enterprise committed to solving the problem of underemployment among men and women with criminal backgrounds. We do this by providing reentry services, job training, and three-month transitional jobs in our honey and natural skincare business, beelove®.

NLEN is committed to:

  • Empowering job seekers to secure stable, family-sustaining employment
  • Partnering with employers to recruit and retain reliable, skilled talent
  • Advocating for equity and fair-change employment in hiring and access to economic opportunity
  • Providing financial education and coaching to help residents build wealth
  • Investing in a thriving, resilient North Lawndale community
  • Revitalizing the North Lawndale community and reducing the community’s unemployment rate by 10% by 2027

We believe in fair chances — not just fair chances — and we’ve seen firsthand the power of employment to restore dignity and change lives. In fact, more than 20 percent of NLEN’s full-time staff are program graduates.

NLEN’s President & CEO, Brenda M. Palms

Our Mission

The North Lawndale Employment Network’s mission is to improve the earnings potential of the North Lawndale community through innovative employment initiatives that lead to economic advancement and an improved quality of life.

Sweet Beginnings, LLCA
Triple Bottom Line Social Enterprise

As one of NLEN’s social enterprises, Sweet Beginnings has a triple-bottom line: people, product, and planet. Sweet Beginnings creates transitional job opportunities while producing high-quality, natural skincare products under the beelove® brand.

Economic Revitalization

Creating sustainable jobs that contribute to North Lawndale’s renewal.

Social Impact

Providing individuals with criminal records a real chance to build a work history and reenter the workforce with confidence.

Quality Product

Producing and selling all-natural honey and honey-infused skincare products that are as good for your skin as they are for the community.

Our Impact Statement

Given the high rate of poverty resulting from racial and economic wealth disparities, we serve the unemployed and asset-limited, income-constrained employed populations in North Lawndale. We help them to secure employment within one year, and develop the underlying skills and behaviors that improve their ability to successfully navigate the job market, secure sustained employment, close educational gaps, and/or improve household economic conditions.

Our Values

We are deeply committed to equity, dignity, and community transformation.

These core values guide everything we do.

Neighborhood-focused employment initiatives are fundamental.

  • Successful programs are neighborhood-focused and community-driven so they meet the specific needs of individuals.

Quality of work matters.

  • People who work hard should not be poor. A full-time job must meet basic needs and offer conditions for engagement, advancement, and respect.

Economic mobility is essential to reducing poverty.

  • Family-supporting wages and financial literacy are core to addressing economic insecurity, which disproportionately affects African-American, Latino, and other families of color. Economic insecurity is not only impacting the poor; it has grown to threaten the middle class. Self-employment and entrepreneurship are critically important economic mobility strategies to generate income and boost household finances.

We must address the racial wealth gap and social inequality.

  • The United States’ history of racially charged public policy has created the modern racial wealth divide not just through the legacy of slavery and Jim Crow, but through the more recent race-based discrimination in hiring, housing subsidies, tax subsidies, and veteran benefits as well as other implicitly and explicitly racist public policies.

Everyone deserves human dignity and empowerment.

  • To uphold this belief, we do all of our work in a manner that values human dignity and eliminates shame,humiliation, and stigma by building on the strengths that help our participants and community survive loss and trauma and rebalance the power dynamic between participants and authority figures.

Research drives our impact.

Through data collection, community insights, and collaborative studies, our research informs program design, strengthens advocacy, and ensures we're meeting the real needs of North Lawndale residents. By grounding our work in evidence, we advance solutions that are both effective and equitable - building a stronger, more resilient community from the ground up.
