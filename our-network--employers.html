Hire talent. Empower change.

At NLEN, we prepare motivated, job-ready individuals who are eager to contribute, grow, and succeed. Our program graduates are equipped with real-world skills, professional training, and the determination to make an impact — on your team, and in their lives.

When you hire from NLEN, you’re not just filling open positions. You’re helping to close the opportunity gap, reduce recidivism, and create a more inclusive, resilient workforce.

Why hire NLEN graduates?

NLEN offers comprehensive staffing support, including pre-screening, CASA and drug testing, skills training, and on-the-job coaching. Our graduates are reliable, workplace-ready, and equipped with the soft skills to thrive in team environments and align with company culture. We’ll also help you tap into hiring incentives that grow your business and bottom line.

Financial Hiring Incentives

When you partner with NLEN, we can help you access a wide range of hiring incentives that best suit your business — and help grow your bottom line.

Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC)

Reduces employers’ tax liability by up to $2,400 to $9,600 per qualifying hire. Under this program, employers can be reimbursed for 100% of the transitional worker wages and employer payroll taxes, including Social Security, Medicare, SUTA (The State Unemployment Tax Act), and FUTE (The Federal Unemployment Tax Act).

Federal Bonding Program

Covers the first six months of a qualifying employee’s employment up to $5,000 with no deductible, and is free of charge to the employer.

Enterprise Zone Job Tax Credit

Allows any employer located in the state of Illinois’ 95 enterprise zones a $500 credit on Illinois income taxes for each hire of an economically disadvantaged individual or dislocated worker.

On-The-Job Training

Employers can get up to 50% reimbursement of a new employee’s wages while they are in training for qualifying jobs.

For more information about subsidized wages for transitional workers, read our Business Solutions Memorandum of Understanding.
